What is uwb hacks from home?

UWB Hacks from Home is a Hackathon hosted by the UW Bothell chapter of the ACM! This weekend long event will be going from Friday, April 9th to Sunday, April 11th. Participants can work in a team, try new things, and choose to build a program which helps everyone deal with working from home. Projects should fall into one of the four tracks: Health, Interact, Tools, or Wildcard, which will be showcased at the end of the event to peers and judges. Register now to make sure you don’t miss anything!

Tracks

Health

Explore ways of helping others deal with their health while stuck at home. If you’ve got a way to help people keep healthy during quarantine, make it real!
Relevant topics: Counseling, Exercise, Journaling, Mental Health, Meditation

Interact

Help people connect with each other, and continue to foster connections. Show us a new way of keeping in contact with the world around us!
Relevant topics: News, Media, Chat-Client, Multiplayer

Tools

Design and build tools to help others develop software or achieve goals while working from home. If you’ve got an idea for a tech tool others can use, we want to see it!
Relevant topics: API, Automation, Bots, Tooling

Wildcard

Got an idea that doesn’t quite fit? Go wild, then! Anything safe-for-work goes, and we’re excited to see where your creativity leads you.
Relevant topics: Games, Art, Sports, Nature, Skateboard
