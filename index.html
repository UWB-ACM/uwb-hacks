About UWB Hacks

This year’s event has a theme of Cloud Computing! We are excited to see all of the innovative projects students will come up with.

Technical Tidbits

On the first day of the event (Friday April 17th), all of the following resources will be available to all attendees:

  • Project mentors to work with students
  • Introductory presentations for users who are new to the cloud
  • Lightning talks given by technologists from Microsoft, Amazon, and UWB

Fun All Weekend Long

Happy hackers make good projects. To that end, expect social events throughout the weekend as well as prizes and swag to keep you motivated!

  • Bingo, Minecraft, and other social events facilitated through Zoom and Discord
  • Team formation mixer workshop on Friday
  • Goodie bags for all registered attendees
  • Prizes and awards for three project categories

A UW Bothell Tradition

UWB Hacks the Cloud is the third annual hackathon at UW Bothell. Check out last year’s event page for more information about the Web Development hackathon and check out what our attendees created! You can also read about the 2018 hackathon on the school news site.

Workshops

Workshops and talks will be held throughout the day on Friday, April 17th, to help attendees find teams, get up to speed on cloud computing concepts, and get inspired by subject matter experts.

  • A workshop for team formation on Friday, presented by UWB's ACM-Women chapter
  • An introduction to cloud computing, presented by the ACM hackathon organizers
  • Lightning talks from industry partners discussing cloud computing products, projects, and paradigms
  • Security-specific presentations (more information coming soon!)

Check out the event schedule for the talk schedule and videoconferencing links.

Getting One-on-One Help

Industry mentors will be standing by on Discord to help individual teams brainstorm, debug, and polish their projects on an as-needed basis. Many of the mentor volunteers are UW Bothell alumni, and this year’s cohort draws employees from the following companies:

• Microsoft• Amazon• T-Mobile
• Google• Expedia• And more!

Join the Discord server to connect with mentors, fellow hackers, and event organizers!

Frequently Asked Questions!

What about COVID-19?

UWB Hacks the Cloud will still be held! It will be completely digital, and the organizers are working hard to make it a highlight of your regional quarantine experience.

The event will start as scheduled at 9:30 AM on Friday, April 17th, 2020. The deadline for project submissions has been extended to Sunday, April 19th at 2pm.

All workshops, technical talks, and presentations will be facilitated through Zoom. Check out the event schedule for the videoconferencing schedule and invite links.

All interested students in the Puget Sound are welcome to attend the hackathon. One small silver lining is that there is no longer a registration cap, so if you want to participate, sign up!

Changelog:

  1. March 13: Created FAQ about COVID-19 and virtual event
  2. March 24: Hackathon dates extended to April 19th
  3. March 24: Videoconferencing links added

What’s a hackathon?

From Wikipedia:

A hackathon is a design sprint-like event in which computer programmers and others involved in software development, including graphic designers, interface designers, project managers, and others, often including domain experts, collaborate intensively on software projects.

What's a hacker?

From Washington Post:

Contrary to popular terminology, a "hacker" does not commit digital mischief..."Those are 'crackers.' Hackers build things, crackers break them." Hackers are the most ardent of computer users, not content to master certain crafts but striving for full and near-spiritual immersion.

What if I don’t have any experience with cloud computing?

That is perfectly fine! The hackathon organizers have planned informative talks, assembled documentation pages, and created demo projects to help get you started.

Check out the event schedule for information about the talks and workshops; look for cloud technology guides, external documentation, and example projects on the documentation page.

Project mentors will be available on Discord on an as-needed basis to help you with your project.

Do I need to be in a specific major?

Not at all! This event is open to all students.

Are prizes offered for team projects?

Yes! We will have 3 prize categories this year:

  • Most Innovative: The project which combines cloud services in innovative and technically advanced ways. Awarded by judges and organizers.
  • Most Impactful: The project which facilitates community awareness, social inclusion, or otherwise improves the lives of your peers. Awarded by judges and organizers.
  • Student Favorite: Awarded by your peers as the best or most popular project.

Each of these categories will come with a prize pack, which will include a mix of IoT devices, DigitalOcean credits, merchandise, and a custom trophy.

More details will be announced at the opening ceremony.

What if I don’t have a team lined up?

There will be a workshop directly after the opening ceremony to help you form a team if you don’t have one at that time.

Do I have to work on a hackathon project?

We want everyone at this event to learn new technologies and collaborate on new ideas. These events are a valuable time to do these things instead of your homework or personal projects.

If you don’t plan on working on a hackathon project, the organizers kindly request that you let us know so we can provide swag and event merchandise to another student who wants to participate.
